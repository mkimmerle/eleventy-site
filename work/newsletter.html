Reducing Child Care Costs

Get quality care for a reasonable cost

 

Child care is costly. Here are some tips that may help you reduce child care costs, without sacrificing quality:

  • Understand the different child care options available. Depending on your situation and needs, certain options may be more cost-effective.
  • Learn more about tax-breaks for child care, such as Dependent Care Assistance Programs (DCAPs), the Child and Dependent Care Credit (CDCC) and the Earned Income Tax Credit (EITC).
  • Find out about public subsidies and scholarships that may be available.
  • Discover other money saving tips.
 
 
 
Identity Theft

Don't be a victim!

 

Identity theft can destroy your credit and ruin your good name. Don't be a victim! Learn how identity theft occurs. Much of our personal information is stored and exchanged via computer -- where it is vulnerable to tech-savvy identity thieves. It is critical to keep your computer safe. Know the signs your identity may have been stolen and what to do if you have fallen prey to an identity thief.

Winter Safety

On the road or at home

 

Winter driving is a big concern for those in snow prone regions. To ensure that you are as safe as possible when on the road in winter weather special safety precautions need to be taken. Exposure to cold temperatures can cause serious health problems. To keep yourself and your family safe, you should know how to prevent cold-related health problems and what to do if a cold-weather health emergency arises. Staying warm inside? December, January and February are the peak months for reported home structure fires and home fire deaths. Consider fire safety precautions and make sure you have working smoke alarms!
